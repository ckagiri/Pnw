Game Overview

The concept of the game is to accurately predict the correct result or score of a football match.

Points are awarded based on how close your prediction is to the actual score of the match.

The objective of the game is to collect as many points as possible and compete for trophies, rewards and prizes.

Scoring

Points are awarded as follows:

1. Correct Score - Correctly predicting the team-score of a match earns you 1 point. If you get both team-scores correct you earn an extra (bonus) point i.e. you earn 3 points for predicting the exact scores of a match.

2. Correct Result - Correctly predicting the outcome of a match i.e. draw or winning team earns you 3 points.

Example:

Team A vs Team B.  Final result 3 - 1 (Team A scored 3 goals, Team B scored 1 goal)

(a) If you predicted 0 – 0, you earn 0 points (You did not predict the correct score for either Team A or Team B, and you did not predict the correct winning team)

(b) If you predicted 0 – 1, you earn 1 point (You predicted the correct score for one team by predicting that Team B will score 1 goal, but you did not predict the correct winning team)

(c) If you predicted 1 – 0, you earn 3 points (You did not predict the correct score for either Team A or Team B, but you predicted that team A will win (correct outcome))

(d) If you predicted 2 – 1, you earn 4 points (You predicted the correct score for 1 team by predicting that Team B will score 1 goal and you also predicted the correct winning team)

(e) If you predicted 3 – 0, you earn 4 points (You predicted the correct score for 1 team by predicting that Team A will score 3 goals and you also predicted the correct winning team)

(f) If you predicted 3 – 1, you earn 6 points (You predicted the correct score for both teams, and you predicted the correct winning team)

In the event of two or more players having the same number of points, the tie break process (see below) is used to decide who ranks higher than the other.

The final ranking of each player will be decided according to the following hierarchy of rules:

1. Greater number of points

2. Greater number of correct score points

3. Greater number of correct result points

4. Greater number of cross product points (see below)

5. Least spread difference (see below)

6. Least accuracy difference (see below)

7. The player with the earlier prediction date/time.

Cross Product Points

- The product between correct score points and correct result points for a given prediction e.g. If for a given prediction you get 1 point for correct score and 3 points for correct result then cross product points = 3.

Spread Difference

- The difference in goal-margin between your prediction and the final match result. (Note: always expressed as 0 or negative number)

Example: Team A vs Team B.  Final result 2 - 1.

If you predicted 3 - 1 : spread-difference= -1. (You predicted Team A will beat Team B by 2 goals, but Team A won by a 1 goal margin)

If you predicted 1 - 0 : spread-difference = 0. (You predicted Team A will beat Team B by 1 goal and sure enough Team A won by a 1 goal margin)

Accuracy Difference

- The number of goals scored and not scored between your prediction and the final match result. (Note: always expressed as 0 or negative number)

Example: Team A vs Team B.  Final result 2 - 1.

If you predicted 3 - 1 : accuracy-difference= -1. (Team A scored a goal less, Team B score was exact)

If you predicted 1 - 0 : accuracy-difference = -2. (Team A scored a goal more and Team B scored a goal more)
